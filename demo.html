Experiment 2 — Calibration Node Deployment

Research question: Where should calibration nodes go to maximise recovery?
h=30 (p_out=0.02), attack=10 seeds. Four placement strategies across calibration counts 0–10. Key finding: Concentrated in attacked group drops final infection 0.88→0.08 at 10 nodes. Placing nodes in the other group does almost nothing — the homophily bottleneck blocks their recovery signal.

Experiment 3 — Attack Robustness & Cascade Threshold

Research question: At what intensity does cascade collapse occur?
K=4, h=12 (p_out=0.05). Sweep attack seeds 1–25, concentrated vs scattered. Key finding: Concentrated cascades at ~8–10 seeds; scattered requires ~15. Concentrating an attack is ~75% more efficient at triggering network-wide collapse.

Experiment 4 — Dynamic Defense Efficiency

Research question: What is the optimal link-severance threshold h_sev?
h=20, attack=10. Dynamic defense severs inter-group edges when out-group infection > h_sev. Key finding: Optimal h_sev = 0.30–0.40 cuts peak from 0.79→0.26 (~67% reduction). Too low severs recovery pathways; too high lets infection through before severance activates.

Experiment 5 — α / β Weight Sensitivity

Research question: How does in-group vs out-group trust shape infection dynamics?
Score = α·r_in + β·r_out. K=4, h=15, attack=8. Key finding: High β=0.8 (out-group trust) delays the threshold cliff to ~0.30–0.35. High α=0.8 (echo chamber) cascades sharply at θ≈0.20. High out-group trust accelerates both infection and recovery.

Experiment 6 — Phase Diagram (h × Attack)

Research question: Where is the exact recovery / collapse boundary?
2D sweep: h ∈ {4…40} × attack ∈ {1…20}. White line = h_crit boundary (10% infection). Key finding: Sharp phase transition. h_crit decreases as attack grows. Click any cell to inspect that parameter combination.

Experiment 7 — Spectral Analysis (λ₂ vs Infection)

Research question: Does algebraic connectivity λ₂ predict infection severity?
Sweep p_out ∈ {0.01…0.20}, measure λ₂ and peak infection for each. Key finding: Pearson r = −0.89, p = 0.0002 (★★★) — strongest statistical result in the project. Fiedler theorem confirmed: lower λ₂ = slower mixing = errors trapped in squad.

Experiment 9 — Inter-Group Disparity (Topic 6 Gaming Gap)

Topic 6 requirement: ΔU = Ū_C1 − Ū_C2 · how unequally are communities affected?
Attack = 8 seeds in Squad 0 (C1). Measure infection in Squad 0 vs others (C2) as h varies. Key finding: At high h, Squad 0 stays infected while other squads recover to near zero. Homophily traps errors in the source community — a structural fairness gap.

Experiment 10 — Feedback Loop (Topic 6 Phase 3)

Topic 6 Phase 3: T-round retrain loop · static vs adaptive defense
Attack = 5 seeds/round for T=10 rounds. Static: fixed threshold. Adaptive: threshold tightens each round based on observed infection (the "classifier retrain" analog). Key finding: At h=20, adaptive defense reduces Squad 0 infection 0.76→0.06 by round 10. Static defense diverges — errors accumulate with no convergence.
